Top Ayurvedic Herbs & Supplements

These are the primary herbs and formulations our Vaidyas prescribe for weight management

Guggul (Commiphora Mukul)

A potent resin known for "scraping" fat from tissues and stimulating the thyroid to boost metabolism.

✓ Stimulates metabolism
✓ Breaks down stubborn body fat
✓ Supports thyroid function
⚠ Avoid during pregnancy

Triphala (Three Fruits)

A combination of three fruits (Amla, Haritaki, Bibhitaki) that acts as a natural laxative and improves digestive health.

✓ Natural detox & laxative
✓ Improves digestion
✓ Rich in antioxidants
⚠ Start with small dose

Trikatu (Three Pungents)

A mix of ginger, black pepper, and long pepper that aids in reducing metabolic waste and enhancing digestion.

✓ Enhances digestion
✓ Reduces metabolic waste
✓ Boosts nutrient absorption
⚠ May cause heat in body

Medohar Guggulu (Classical Tablet)

A classical Ayurvedic tablet often used for weight management. Combines multiple herbs for synergistic fat-reducing effect.

✓ Classical weight management formula
✓ Reduces cholesterol
✓ Balances Kapha dosha
⚠ Consult Vaidya for dosage

Punarnava (Boerhavia Diffusa)

Acting as a natural diuretic, it is highly effective for reducing weight caused by water retention and bloating.

✓ Reduces water retention
✓ Anti-inflammatory
✓ Supports kidney function
⚠ Monitor with kidney conditions

Fenugreek (Methi Seeds)

High in fiber, these seeds reduce hunger and help the body excrete fat. Also regulates blood sugar.

✓ Promotes satiety
✓ Blood sugar regulation
✓ High fiber content
⚠ May interact with diabetes meds

Garcinia Cambogia (Vrikshamla)

A single-herb supplement that blocks fat production and naturally suppresses appetite via HCA (hydroxycitric acid).

✓ Appetite suppression
✓ Blocks fat production
✓ Boosts serotonin
⚠ Avoid with liver conditions

Single Herbs & Spices for Weight Loss

Contains curcumin, which reduces inflammation linked to obesity and helps the liver process fat efficiently.

Black Pepper (Kali Mirch)

Contains piperine, which speeds up metabolic rate and helps break down fat cells.

Cumin (Jeera)

Stimulates digestive enzymes and accelerates the metabolic rate for effective weight management.

Ashwagandha (Winter Cherry)

Lowers cortisol (stress hormone), preventing stress-related weight gain around the abdomen.

Aloe Vera (Kumari)

Drinking its juice on an empty stomach helps detoxify the digestive tract and prevents fat accumulation.

Amla (Indian Gooseberry)

A superfood that flushes out toxins and keeps the digestive system running smoothly. Rich in Vitamin C.

Eat Right, Lose Weight

Dietary Tips for Weight Loss

Ayurvedic food recommendations that complement your herbal treatment

🥛

Buttermilk (Takra)

Consume thin buttermilk with cumin and black pepper to support metabolism. Best taken after lunch.

🍵

Warm Water / Ginger Tea

Drinking warm water throughout the day aids in burning fat. Adding ginger amplifies thermogenic effect.

🍯

Honey (Madhu)

Limited consumption of honey with warm water and lemon is considered a "scraping" agent for old fat.

🏃

Regular Activity

Daily exercise to ensure sweating is emphasized. Sweating is one of Ayurveda's key detox mechanisms.

Daily Habits

Single Lifestyle Actions

In Ayurveda, simple daily habits are just as important as herbs

💧

Lukewarm Water

Drinking warm water throughout the day, especially first thing in the morning, helps "melt" fat and jumpstart digestion. Your Agni (digestive fire) needs warmth to function optimally.

🍯

Honey + Warm Water

When taken with warm water and lemon, honey is considered a "scraping" agent for old, accumulated fat (Ama). Take it every morning on an empty stomach for best results.

🚶

Brisk Walking (Shatapavali)

Ayurveda recommends a short walk after every meal to ensure proper digestion and prevent fat storage. Even 15 minutes after lunch can make a significant difference.

😴

Avoid Daytime Sleep

Sleeping during the day (Divaswapna) is one of the primary causes of Kapha accumulation and weight gain in Ayurveda. Stay active during Kapha hours (6 AM - 10 AM).
